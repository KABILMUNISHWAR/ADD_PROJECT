Exploring Kerala - God's Own Country

About Kerala

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is often referred to as "God's Own Country" due to its stunning natural beauty, unique culture, and rich history. From the tranquil backwaters of Alappuzha to the high hills of Munnar, Kerala offers a diverse range of experiences for all kinds of travelers. Whether you want to relax on a beach, explore lush tea plantations, or experience the vibrant festivals, Kerala has something for everyone.

Top Places to Visit in Kerala

  • Alappuzha (Alleppey): Known for its serene backwaters and houseboat cruises, Alappuzha is a must-visit destination for nature lovers and those seeking tranquility.
  • Munnar: Famous for its sprawling tea plantations, mist-covered hills, and lush landscapes, Munnar is a perfect escape for mountain lovers and trekkers.
  • Kumarakom: Located near Vembanad Lake, Kumarakom offers beautiful views, boat rides, and an abundance of birdlife, making it an ideal spot for relaxation and nature walks.
  • Fort Kochi: A blend of colonial architecture and vibrant culture, Fort Kochi offers a glimpse into Kerala's rich past with its historic churches, Chinese fishing nets, and cultural events.
  • Varkala: A beach town that attracts both spiritual seekers and travelers looking for a laid-back beach experience. Its cliffs overlooking the Arabian Sea make it unique.
  • Wayanad: Known for its wildlife sanctuaries, waterfalls, and trekking spots, Wayanad is a haven for adventure lovers and nature enthusiasts.
  • Thekkady: Famous for the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Thekkady offers opportunities for boating, jungle walks, and elephant rides, providing an unforgettable wildlife experience.

My Kerala Experience

My journey through Kerala was nothing short of magical. I began my adventure in the backwaters of Alappuzha, where I spent a night aboard a traditional houseboat. The calm waters and lush green surroundings created a peaceful atmosphere that was perfect for unwinding. Next, I headed to Munnar, where I explored the rolling tea plantations and enjoyed the cool, refreshing air.

In Fort Kochi, I marveled at the colonial architecture, walked along the charming streets, and tasted the local seafood delicacies. The highlight of my trip was a visit to Varkala Beach, where I relaxed by the cliffs and enjoyed a beautiful sunset over the Arabian Sea. Kerala truly lives up to its reputation as a land of stunning beauty and cultural richness.

Things to Do in Kerala

  • Experience a traditional **Houseboat Cruise** in Alappuzha or Kumarakom.
  • Relax on the beautiful **beaches of Varkala and Kovalam**.
  • Explore the **tea plantations in Munnar** and take a walk through the hills.
  • Go on a **wildlife safari in Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary** in Thekkady.
  • Visit the **historic Fort Kochi**, with its ancient churches, Chinese fishing nets, and vibrant art scene.
  • Take a boat ride to **Sabarimala** or explore **Vembanad Lake**.
  • Attend the famous **Onam festival** or experience the traditional **Kathakali dance performance**.
  • Visit the **Athirappilly Waterfalls**, often called the "Niagara of India."

Travel Tips for Kerala

If you’re planning a trip to Kerala, here are some tips to make the most of your visit:

  • Try to visit during the cooler months (November to March) to avoid the monsoon rains and enjoy the best weather.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and shoes, especially if you plan to explore the hill stations or go trekking.
  • Don't miss out on the local cuisine, especially the seafood. Be sure to try **Kerala Sadya**, a traditional vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf.
  • If you’re visiting beaches, carry sunscreen and swimwear. For temple visits, make sure you follow the dress code (modest attire).
  • Book houseboats and accommodations in advance, especially during peak tourist seasons, as they can get fully booked.
