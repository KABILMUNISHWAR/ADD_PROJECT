Onam Festival - A Celebration of Kerala

What is Onam?

Onam is one of the most important festivals in Kerala, celebrated with immense enthusiasm and joy. It marks the annual visit of the legendary King Mahabali, who is believed to return to Kerala during this festival. Onam usually falls in the month of Chingam in the Malayalam calendar, which corresponds to the months of August and September.

Traditions and Celebrations

Onam is celebrated through various traditional activities such as:

  • Pookalam - Floral decorations in front of homes.
  • Onasadya - A grand feast with an array of traditional Kerala dishes served on a banana leaf.
  • Vallamkali - The famous boat races in Kerala's backwaters.
  • Thiruvathira Kali - A traditional dance performed by women.
  • Onam Games - Various fun games like tug-of-war, archery, and more.

Significance of Onam

The festival symbolizes the victory of good over evil and the spirit of unity, equality, and prosperity. The homecoming of King Mahabali brings with it a sense of renewal and the hope for a better future for all. During Onam, people come together, forgetting differences, and celebrate in harmony with their loved ones.
